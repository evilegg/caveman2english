=== SN-ROUGE-1 FINDING ===

sn-ROUGE-1 strips sentence-initial deontic modal prefixes ("you should", "you must",
"you need to", "you ought to", "you should feel free to", "you might want to") from
both hypothesis and reference before scoring.  This removes the unigram penalty for
imperative conversion ("You should wrap X" → "Wrap X").

Gilfoyle sn-ROUGE-1 = 73.3% vs standard ROUGE-1 = 72.0%.

The 1.3-point gain shows modal stripping accounts for a small fraction of the gap.
The remaining gap vs RFC (84.7%) is driven by Gilfoyle's abbreviation vocabulary:
"DB", "req", "conn" in the output don't match "database", "request", "connection"
in the original at the unigram level.  RFC+c2e outputs pass through the c2e expander
which expands these abbreviations, but Gilfoyle is intentionally left as direct-read
output with no post-processing step.

Two interpretations:
  1. Gilfoyle's ROUGE-1 gap is mostly a vocabulary mismatch artefact (abbreviations),
     not a restructuring artefact (modal/imperative conversion).
  2. sn-ROUGE-1 is still a more accurate measure of restructuring fidelity than
     standard ROUGE-1, even though it does not close the full gap.

A third normalisation step -- expanding abbreviations in both hypothesis and reference
before scoring -- would likely close most of the remaining gap.  This is a v3.1 candidate.

=== METRIC NOTES ===

Compression (original corpus: 14.1%):
  v2 ceiling on the original corpus. Neither encoder strips verbs.
  Real caveman saves 65-75% by stripping verbs.

Compression (verbose corpus: 32.2%):
  v3 pre-pass has abundant surface on verbose corporate/LLM prose.
  Primary drivers: "it is X that Y" stripping (~12-15% per entry),
  verbose connective compression ("in the event that" → "if", etc.),
  quantifier reduction ("all of the" → "all"), and verbose verb phrases
  ("make use of" → "use"). Genitive inversion and nominalisation reversal
  each contribute 2-4% per entry.

ROUGE-1 (verbose corpus: 54.9%):
  Lower than original corpus (72%) because v3 makes more structural changes.
  "It is important to note that X" → X strips many unigrams from overlap.
  The metric penalises every structural change even when no information is lost.

sn-ROUGE-1 (original corpus: 73.3%):
  Strips deontic modal prefixes before scoring.  Accounts for 1.3% of the gap.
  The remaining gap vs RFC is mostly abbreviation vocabulary mismatch (see above).

Modal recovery (verbose corpus: 97.9%):
  One entry (incident-race) dropped to 75% -- the "it is possible that"
  catch-all stripped the uncertainty framing before tilde hedging could
  re-encode it. An issue for v3.1: apply tilde first when stripping filler
  constructions that contain genuine uncertainty markers.

Causal recovery (100% -- consistent across all corpora and versions).

Reconstruction-required (0% -- consistent):
  No detectable broken bindings in either corpus or either version.
